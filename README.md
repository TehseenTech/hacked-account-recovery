# Hacked WhatsApp & Social Account Recovery

> Educational, safety-first guidance for people responding to cyber incidents. This project is independent and is not a law-enforcement agency, law firm, bank, telecom operator, or account-recovery service.

A practical recovery and containment guide for compromised WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, Google, and other social-media accounts.

## If the incident is happening now

- Use the platform's official recovery flow from a trusted device; never use recovery links sent by strangers.
- Secure the primary email account first, then change reused passwords on other services.
- Review active sessions, linked devices, recovery email addresses, phone numbers, app passwords, and connected third-party apps.
- Enable multi-factor authentication and save recovery codes offline.
- Warn contacts through a separate trusted channel if the attacker may be impersonating you.
- Preserve alerts, login notifications, attacker messages, profile URLs, and recovery case numbers before removing them.

## Evidence checklist

- Login alerts and security emails with full headers when available
- Screenshots of changed profile details, linked devices, and unauthorized posts
- Platform username, numeric profile ID when available, and exact profile URL
- Dates, times, IP/location notices, recovery attempts, and support ticket numbers

## What not to do

- Never share an OTP, six-digit registration code, password, or backup code.
- Do not pay unofficial 'hackers' or recovery agents.
- Do not repeatedly submit contradictory recovery forms; maintain one accurate incident timeline.

## Simple incident timeline

| Date and time | Time zone | Event | Account or URL | Evidence file | Action taken | Reference number |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM | PKT / UTC+5 | What happened | Identifier | File name | Report or containment step | Ticket / complaint ID |

Keep facts separate from assumptions. Record times exactly as displayed and note the time zone. Preserve original files; use copies for review or redaction.

## Reporting in Pakistan

For cybercrime complaints, use the official [NCCIA complaint portal](https://complaint.nccia.gov.pk/) or the current reporting methods listed on the [NCCIA website](https://www.nccia.gov.pk/). For immediate physical danger, contact local emergency services. For urgent financial loss, contact the relevant bank, wallet, card issuer, or payment provider first.

## Privacy and repository safety

This is a public repository. **Do not open an issue containing victim names, CNIC or passport numbers, phone numbers, financial details, credentials, private media, chat exports, case files, or other evidence.** Use official private reporting channels.
